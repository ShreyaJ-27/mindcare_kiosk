üíö Remember: Mental Health is Health

Taking care of your mental health is just as important as taking care of your physical health. It's okay to not be okay sometimes, and it's always okay to ask for help. You deserve support, understanding, and compassion - especially from yourself.

üò∞ Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress. While everyone feels anxious sometimes, an anxiety disorder involves excessive worry that interferes with daily life.

Common Signs of Anxiety:

  • Constant worrying that's hard to control
  • Feeling restless or on edge
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Muscle tension or headaches
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Racing heart or rapid breathing
  • Avoiding situations that make you anxious

üí° What You Can Do:

Practice deep breathing, use grounding techniques, limit caffeine, exercise regularly, talk to someone you trust, and consider professional help if anxiety interferes with your life.

Remember: Anxiety is treatable. With the right support and coping strategies, you can manage anxiety and live a full, happy life.

üòî Understanding Depression

Depression is more than just feeling sad. It's a medical condition that affects how you think, feel, and function in daily life. Depression is common, real, and treatable.

Common Signs of Depression:

  • Persistent sad, empty, or hopeless feelings
  • Loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Low energy and fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

üí° What You Can Do:

Talk to a trusted adult, counselor, or doctor. Depression is highly treatable with therapy, medication, or both. Small steps like exercise, good sleep, and social connection also help.

Important: If you have thoughts of suicide, please reach out immediately. Call a crisis helpline or go to an emergency room. You deserve help and support.

üò£ Managing Stress

Stress is your body's reaction to challenges or demands. While some stress is normal, too much stress over time can harm your mental and physical health.

Signs You're Too Stressed:

  • Feeling overwhelmed or unable to cope
  • Irritability or mood swings
  • Difficulty relaxing or sleeping
  • Headaches or muscle tension
  • Changes in appetite
  • Withdrawing from others
  • Difficulty concentrating

üí° Healthy Ways to Manage Stress:

Exercise regularly, practice relaxation techniques, get enough sleep, eat well, connect with friends and family, set boundaries, take breaks, and don't be afraid to say "no" sometimes.

Quick Stress Relief:

  • Take a 5-minute walk
  • Practice deep breathing
  • Listen to calming music
  • Talk to a friend
  • Write in a journal
  • Do something you enjoy

üò¥ Sleep & Mental Health

Good sleep is essential for mental health. Poor sleep can make anxiety and depression worse, while mental health problems can make it harder to sleep.

How Much Sleep Do You Need?

  • Teenagers (14-17): 8-10 hours per night
  • Young Adults (18-25): 7-9 hours per night
  • Adults (26+): 7-9 hours per night

üí° Tips for Better Sleep:

Keep a regular sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, avoid screens 1 hour before bed, keep your room dark and cool, avoid caffeine in the evening, and exercise during the day (not close to bedtime).

When to Seek Help:

  • You regularly can't fall asleep or stay asleep
  • You feel tired even after sleeping
  • Sleep problems affect your daily life
  • You snore loudly or gasp during sleep

Remember: If sleep problems persist, talk to a doctor. Sleep disorders are treatable.

üå∏ Self-Care Basics

Self-care means taking time to do things that help you live well and improve your mental and physical health. It's not selfish - it's necessary.

Physical Self-Care:

  • Eat nutritious meals regularly
  • Exercise or move your body
  • Get enough sleep
  • Stay hydrated
  • Attend medical appointments

Emotional Self-Care:

  • Express your feelings in healthy ways
  • Practice self-compassion
  • Set boundaries with others
  • Do things you enjoy
  • Celebrate small victories

Social Self-Care:

  • Spend time with people who support you
  • Ask for help when you need it
  • Say no to things that drain you
  • Join groups with similar interests

üí° Daily Self-Care Ideas:

Take a walk, read for pleasure, listen to music, practice gratitude, try a new hobby, spend time in nature, call a friend, take a relaxing bath, or simply rest.

Remember: Self-care looks different for everyone. Find what works for you and make it a priority.

ü§ù Getting Help

Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Many people need support for their mental health, and treatment works.

Who Can Help:

  • School Counselor: Free, confidential support at school
  • Therapist/Psychologist: Trained to help with mental health
  • Psychiatrist: Medical doctor who can prescribe medication
  • Primary Care Doctor: Can assess and refer you
  • Crisis Helplines: Immediate support when you need it

üí° How to Start the Conversation:

"I've been struggling with my mental health and I think I need help." or "I haven't been feeling like myself lately. Can we talk?" or "I'm having a hard time and could use support."

What to Expect in Therapy:

  • A safe, confidential space to talk
  • No judgment - therapists are there to help
  • Learning coping skills and strategies
  • Understanding your thoughts and feelings better
  • Progress takes time - be patient with yourself

Remember: Finding the right therapist might take time. It's okay to try different people until you find a good fit.
